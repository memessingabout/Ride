About BODDA

The Digital Boda Drivers and Deliveries Association (BODDA) is revolutionizing Kenya's transport sector through technology and collective action.

Founded in 2020, we've grown to become the largest and most influential boda boda association in Kenya, representing riders across all 47 counties. Our mission is to professionalize the sector, improve working conditions, and leverage digital solutions to increase earnings and safety.

The Boda Boda Journey

A timeline of motorcycle transport in Kenya

1960s

Origins at the Border

The term "boda boda" originated from bicycle taxis operating at the Kenya-Uganda border, transporting people between border posts ("border-to-border").

1990s

Motorcycle Adoption

Bicycles began being replaced with motorcycles, offering faster transport. The industry grew rapidly in urban and rural areas as an affordable alternative to matatus.

2000s

National Phenomenon

Boda bodas became ubiquitous across Kenya, serving as vital transport in areas with poor road infrastructure. The sector became a major employer of youth.

2010s

Digital Transformation

Ride-hailing apps like UberBoda and Bolt entered the market, bringing digital dispatch and cashless payments to the industry.

2020

BODDA Formation

The Digital Boda Drivers and Deliveries Association was founded to represent riders' interests and professionalize the sector through technology and advocacy.

Present

Economic Powerhouse

With over 1.2 million riders, the boda boda sector contributes significantly to Kenya's GDP and serves as a critical last-mile delivery solution.
